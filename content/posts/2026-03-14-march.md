Yeah..

We're often told to learn from experienced people.Fair. Experience does something interesting.

It compresses years of mistakes, patterns and observations into something that looks a lot like wisdom.

But here's the thing nobody mentions:None of us runs the same operating system.

Every person moves through a different sequence of events. Different environments. Different risks taken. Different failures survived. Over time those experiences quietly install their o

wn validation rules in our mental software.

So what one person confidently calls "truth" is often just an interpretation that successfully passed through their filters.

That doesn't make it wrong.

It just means it isn't universal.Progress rarely comes from repeating what worked yesterday. More often it starts when someone pauses and asks a slightly uncomfortable question:

What if the default setting is wrong?

Learn from the past. Absolutely.

But don't outsource your thinking to it.bGrowth happens somewhere between inherited wisdom and the courage to challenge it .
